<p><p>The factory process surveillance camera market is segmented into two primary types: infrared and non-infrared cameras. Infrared cameras leverage thermal imaging technology to detect heat signatures, making them ideal for low-light or nighttime surveillance, ensuring continuous monitoring of factory processes. In contrast, non-infrared cameras, such as standard and high-definition cameras, rely on visible light for monitoring, providing clear visual evidence in well-lit environments. Both types are essential for enhancing security, optimizing operations, and ensuring compliance in manufacturing settings.</p></p>
