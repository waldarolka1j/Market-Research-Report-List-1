<p><p>The medical fabrics market comprises three main types: non-woven, woven, and knitted fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are created by bonding fibers through mechanical, thermal, or chemical processes, making them ideal for disposable items like surgical masks and gowns. Woven fabrics are made by interlacing yarns, providing strength and durability, often used in patient gowns and drapes. Knitted fabrics, formed by interlocking loops of yarn, offer stretch and comfort, commonly found in bandages and therapeutic garments, enhancing patient care and functionality.</p></p>
