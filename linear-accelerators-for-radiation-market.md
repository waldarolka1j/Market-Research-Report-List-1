<p><ul><li>Low-energy Linacs</li><li>High-energy Linacs</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Linear accelerators for radiation therapy are categorized into low-energy and high-energy linacs, serving distinct medical needs. Low-energy linacs typically operate below 6 MV and are primarily used for superficial tumors, offering precise doses for localized treatment. Conversely, high-energy linacs, operating at or above 6 MV, are designed for deeper-seated tumors, providing enhanced penetration and more effective dose distribution. The market for these devices reflects their significance in specialized cancer therapies, catering to diverse clinical requirements and technological advancements.</p></p>
