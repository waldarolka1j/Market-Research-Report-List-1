<p><p>The oral cancer diagnosis market utilizes various imaging and diagnostic technologies to detect and evaluate cancer. Computed Tomography (CT) scans provide detailed cross-sectional images, while Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) offers superior soft tissue contrast. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) helps identify metabolic activity of tumors. Ultrasonography uses sound waves for real-time imaging, and biopsies confirm the presence of cancerous cells. X-rays visualize bone involvement, while endoscopy allows direct examination of oral lesions. Fluorescence imaging enhances tissue visualization, alongside other emerging diagnostic methods.</p></p>
