<p><p>The Non-reagent Residual Chlorine Analyzer market features two primary measurement types: colorimetric and polarographic. Colorimetric measurement involves detecting residual chlorine through changes in color intensity, using specific reagents that react with chlorine to produce a measurable color change. In contrast, polarographic measurement utilizes an electrode that responds to chlorine concentrations, generating an electrical signal proportional to the chlorine level. These methods are crucial for water quality monitoring in various applications, ensuring safety and compliance with health standards.</p></p>
