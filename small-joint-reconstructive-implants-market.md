<p><p>The Small Joint Reconstructive Implants market encompasses primarily two types: metal and polymer implants. Metal implants, often made from titanium or cobalt-chromium alloys, offer superior strength and durability, making them ideal for weight-bearing joints. In contrast, polymer implants, typically composed of materials like polyethylene, provide flexibility and are frequently used in less load-bearing applications. Both types aim to restore joint function and alleviate pain, with demand driven by an aging population and increased incidence of joint-related disorders.</p></p>
