<p><p>Vacuum gauges for semiconductor equipment are essential for measuring low-pressure environments. The capacitance diaphragm gauge operates by measuring the deflection of a diaphragm under pressure changes. Ionization vacuum gauges rely on ionization of gas molecules to provide pressure readings in high-vacuum conditions. Pirani vacuum gauges utilize thermal conductivity principles, measuring the heat loss from a heated element to determine pressure. Other types may include needle gauges, vacuum transducers, and various specialized sensors tailored for specific applications in semiconductor fabrication.</p></p>
